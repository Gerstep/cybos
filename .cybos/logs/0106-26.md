## Key Findings

**Core Investment Thesis**: $5-8T AI capex wave creates paradox—massive spending, 95% project failure. This spawns $2-4T in second-order market opportunities not in compute, but in:

1. **Trust & Verification** ($2.1B→$8.5B by 2032)
   - AI output verification, hallucination detection, deepfake detection
   
2. **AI Governance** ($2-3B→$10-15B by 2032)
   - Explainable AI, bias detection, model monitoring
   - "AI Governance OS" as $1-3B TAM opportunity
   
3. **Quality Assurance/Testing** ($500M→$2-5B by 2030)
   - Agentic QA platforms solving 95% failure problem
   
4. **Talent/Reskilling** ($10-15B annual market)
   - $5.5T global cost of skills gap
   - AI engineering bootcamps, verification platforms (Workera)
   
5. **Organizational Restructuring/PE** (3-5x MOIC)
   - $12B Amazon logistics automation
   - $1.5B JPMorgan cost savings from AI
   - 144 large M&A deals in 2025; 25% have AI theme

## Wealth Concentration Finding

AI abundance creates inequality, not prosperity:
- Automation-focused AI favors capital over labor
- Wealth concentration accelerating (Magnificent Seven = 34% of S&P 500)
- High-skill workers benefit; low-skill displaced
- Advanced economies pull ahead of developing world

## Complement Shift

Intelligence commoditizing; value shifting to taste/judgment/curation:
- LLMs generate options; humans judge quality
- Market for taste/curation infrastructure: $100M-1B emerging
- Companies winning: AI + taste + systematic process (Anthropic, Stripe, Figma)
